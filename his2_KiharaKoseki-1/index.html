Introduction

This webpage explores the transborder experience of Japanese immigrants in Mexico during the early 20th century, with a focus on Denzo Jesus Kihara. The materials presented here document the complex intersection of Japanese, Mexican, and American histories during a period of significant global change.

Denzo Kihara emigrated from Kyushu, Japan to Mexico in the early 20th century, settling in Ciudad Juárez along the Mexico-U.S. border. His migration occurred during a pivotal period when U.S. anti-Asian legislation redirected Japanese immigration toward Latin America. During World War II, Kihara was removed from the border region to the Villa Aldama labor camp and later to Mexico City.
